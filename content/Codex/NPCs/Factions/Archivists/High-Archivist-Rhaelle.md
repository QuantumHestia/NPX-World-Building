### I. Public Abstract—Filed for General Access

High Archivist Rhaelle serves as custodian of doctrinal integrity and restricted holdings within the Archives of Eldoria. Her office oversees classification standards, the handling of hazardous texts, and the authorization of field inquiries involving anomalous workings.

Her published work focuses on repeatable settlement behavior following breaches, emphasizing anchors, recognition weight, and conservative handling of high-risk demonstrations.

**Public Doctrine Position (Summary):**
- Magic is predictable *enough* to survive, but never predictable enough to indulge.
- A working is best understood by its **anchors**, its **recognition**, and its **cost of persistence**.
- The Archives exist to prevent private error from becoming public catastrophe.

> [!archivist-note-right]
> A “public abstract” is what you say when the truth would teach the wrong person the wrong lesson.

---

### II. Office Authority (General)

**Authorized Domains:**
- Classification of texts and rites by hazard tier.
- Oversight of oath-registries and verification of binding speech in civic disputes (upon request).
- Arbitration support for temples and colleges when rite-results diverge across jurisdictions.
- Issuance of sealed writs for field observation of anomalies.

**Restrictions:**
- The High Archivist does not authorize public demonstrations of major workings without independent review.
- Reproduction of restricted diagrams, names, or bindings is prohibited outside sanctioned instruction.

---

### III. Publications (General Catalog)

- **The Unified Theory of the Weave** *(Working Draft; sanctioned for study with redactions)*
- **On Anchors and Drift** *(Collected notes on Reversion and Substitution behaviors)*
- **Witness-weight and Civic Risk** *(A memorandum distributed to city councils and temple registries)*

> [!archivist-note-left]
> People imagine we hide knowledge because we are jealous. We hide it because knowledge reproduces faster than wisdom.

---

### IV. Method (Public Description)

Rhaelle’s investigations prioritize:
- convergent accounts from independent witnesses,
- repeatable outcomes under constrained conditions,
- controlled terminology (what can be tested is what is worth naming),
- and containment-first handling of hazardous material.

She is known to discourage “heroic” inquiry in favor of long observation, incremental trials, and broad cross-checking of registries.

---

### V. Reputation (Filed by Third Parties)

**Frequently recorded descriptors:**
- methodical, difficult to impress,
- intolerant of sensational claims,
- protective of apprentices,
- politically careful—especially where temples, crowns, and patrons overlap.

**Contested descriptors:**
- “cold,” “unromantic,” “too cautious,” “more jurist than scholar.”

> [!archivist-note-right]
> “Cold” is what the reckless call the person who refuses to die with them.

---

### VI. Personal Notes

> [!archivist-note-left]
